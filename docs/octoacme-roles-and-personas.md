# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with UX/UI Designers on feasibility and implementation of designs
- Work with DevOps/Release Engineers on CI/CD pipeline integration

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Partner with UX/UI Designers to ensure solutions are user-centered
- Engage Data Analysts/Scientists to define and track success metrics
- Coordinate with Customer Support/Advocacy to incorporate user feedback

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Confirm design review, documentation, security signoff, and support handoff gates are met before release
- Ensure all cross-functional contributors (design, security, support, DevOps) are engaged at the right project stages

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers are responsible for designing user interfaces and experiences that align with user needs and business goals. They ensure that products are intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Iterate designs based on user feedback and usability testing
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure feasible and high-quality implementation
- Establish and maintain design system components and guidelines
- Conduct or facilitate usability reviews before release

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered designs that reduce friction and improve satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency and accessibility across features
- Shorten feedback loops between design and engineering

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers during sprint planning and mid-sprint
- Prototype walkthroughs and usability feedback sessions
- Design spec handoffs via design tools (e.g., Figma)

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers ensure all documentation—user guides, API docs, internal onboarding materials—is clear, accurate, and up-to-date. They translate complex technical details into content that is accessible to the intended audience.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain technical and process documentation
- Coordinate with Developers and Product Managers to capture technical changes
- Edit and review docs for clarity, accuracy, and consistency
- Maintain documentation alongside feature releases
- Support onboarding by keeping internal process docs current

### Goals
- Ensure documentation quality keeps pace with product changes
- Reduce support burden by providing self-service documentation
- Improve onboarding time for new team members and users

### Typical Communication
- Sync with Developers and Product Managers during sprint reviews and before release
- Documentation readiness check-ins as part of the release checklist
- Collaboration on release notes and changelog entries

---

## Customer Support/Advocacy

### Role Summary
Customer Support/Advocacy representatives are the voice of the customer. They surface user feedback, pain points, and support escalations to the project team, ensuring the customer perspective shapes product decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate user issues and recurring pain points to Product and Engineering
- Provide input during requirements gathering and user acceptance testing
- Help refine support and escalation processes for new features
- Review release notes and documentation for customer-facing clarity
- Coordinate with the team to prepare for support impacts of each release

### Goals
- Minimize customer-facing issues at and after release
- Ensure the team has visibility into real-world user impact
- Improve self-service support through better documentation and tooling

### Typical Communication
- Participates in release readiness reviews and post-release retrospectives
- Shares support trends and escalation summaries with Product and PM
- Liaises between users/customers and the project team, especially during incidents and releases

---

## Security/Compliance Lead

### Role Summary
Security/Compliance Leads ensure that security and compliance requirements are identified, addressed, and verified from project planning through delivery. They are the primary escalation point for security risks and regulatory concerns.

### Responsibilities
- Review designs and implementation plans for security risks and compliance gaps
- Participate in threat modeling for significant features or changes
- Review and help resolve reported vulnerabilities
- Ensure compliance with applicable standards (e.g., GDPR, SOC 2, HIPAA) throughout the project lifecycle
- Conduct or coordinate security sign-off prior to release
- Train team members on relevant compliance standards and secure coding practices

### Goals
- Ensure no critical security or compliance issues reach production
- Build a security-aware culture within the team
- Reduce risk exposure through proactive review and remediation

### Typical Communication
- Security design review participation during planning and before major releases
- Vulnerability triage and remediation discussions with Developers and QA
- Compliance status updates to Project Managers and stakeholders
- Sign-off confirmation as part of the release checklist

---

## DevOps/Release Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps/Release Engineers manage deployment pipelines, infrastructure automation, and release processes. They ensure that software can be delivered reliably, repeatably, and with minimal manual intervention.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain deployment tooling, pipelines, and infrastructure-as-code
- Automate build, test, and release processes (CI/CD)
- Monitor environment health, performance, and reliability
- Coordinate deployment windows and rollback procedures with the team
- Ensure staging and production environments are kept in sync
- Support incident response with environment diagnostics and rollback execution

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize manual steps and toil in the release process
- Maintain high environment availability and observability

### Typical Communication
- Sprint and release planning to align on deployment needs and schedules
- CI/CD pipeline status and incident alerts shared with Developers and QA
- Release coordination communications with Project Managers and Customer Support

---

## Data Analyst/Scientist

### Role Summary
Data Analysts/Scientists support data-driven decision-making through analysis, experimentation, and actionable insights. They help the team understand product performance and validate hypotheses.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze product usage, performance, and business data
- Define and track success metrics in collaboration with Product Managers
- Design and interpret experiments to validate hypotheses (e.g., A/B tests, feature rollouts)
- Build and maintain dashboards and reporting tools
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities for product improvement

### Goals
- Ensure decisions are grounded in evidence rather than assumption
- Surface leading indicators of success or risk early
- Enable rapid learning through well-designed experiments

### Typical Communication
- Metric definition sessions with Product Managers during planning
- Data insights shared in sprint reviews and stakeholder updates
- Experiment results and recommendations presented to Product and Engineering leads
